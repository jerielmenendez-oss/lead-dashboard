Executive Summary

Alabama Plumbing & Gas Service Inc. has a website and serves multiple cities across Baldwin and Mobile counties — that’s a real advantage over competitors who have no web presence at all. The problem is that your site has been neglected for years.

The copyright still says 2018. You’re running Universal Analytics, which Google deprecated in July 2023 — meaning you haven’t had working analytics for over two years and have no idea how many visitors find your site or where they come from. There are no meta descriptions (the text snippets that show in Google search results), no JSON-LD schema markup (structured data that tells Google your business info), and the overall design screams “2018 GoDaddy template.” A Facebook Pixel suggests you once ran social ads, but the site itself doesn’t convert that traffic effectively. Your multi-city service area is a major asset — it just needs a modern site to leverage it.

Key Findings

Copyright Stuck at 2018 — Site Looks Abandoned

Your website footer says © 2018. It’s now 2026. When a homeowner sees a copyright date from 8 years ago, they wonder if the business is still operating. This is an easy fix that makes a big psychological difference — but it’s also a symptom of a site that hasn’t been touched in years.

Found: Footer copyright © 2018

Still Running Universal Analytics (Deprecated)

Your site still uses Google’s Universal Analytics (UA), which was shut down in July 2023. This means you have not had working website analytics for over two years. You have no data on how many people visit your site, which pages they view, or how they found you. Migrating to Google Analytics 4 (GA4) is essential.

No Meta Descriptions on Any Page

Meta descriptions are the short text previews that show up in Google search results under your page title. Without them, Google pulls random text from your page, which usually looks messy and doesn’t convince anyone to click. Every page needs a clear, compelling meta description.

No JSON-LD Schema Markup

Schema markup is structured data that tells Google exactly what your business is, where it’s located, what services you offer, and your hours. Without it, Google has to guess — and it often guesses wrong. Adding PlumberService and LocalBusiness schema would help Google display your business info (phone, hours, rating) directly in search results.

GoDaddy Builder Template — Dated Design

The GoDaddy Website Builder template you’re using looks like a 2018-era design. Modern plumbing websites have moved to cleaner layouts with prominent phone numbers, emergency call-to-action buttons, and service area maps. Your site looks noticeably older than competitors.

20 Cities Listed in One Paragraph — No City Pages

Your site mentions 20 service cities in a single block of text. Your competitor S.E. Tradesmen has individual, SEO-optimized pages for each city — Daphne, Foley, Gulf Shores, etc. — and each one captures its own keyword traffic. Building even 5 city-specific pages would be a step-change in organic visibility compared to listing them all in one paragraph.

Competitor Has 213 Five-Star Reviews — You Have None Visible

S.E. Tradesmen Plumbing & Gas serves the same 20-city footprint as you and has 213 five-star Google reviews. That’s the kind of social proof that dominates the local map pack. Without a confirmed Google Business Profile and visible reviews, you simply cannot compete for those top-3 map positions.

Fast Page Load & Mobile Responsive

Your site loads in about 2.2 seconds with a 201ms server response time, and it is mobile responsive with no horizontal scrolling. Your phone number is also prominent in the header on both desktop and mobile. These are genuine positives — the technical foundation is sound, it just needs modern SEO elements layered on top.

Multi-City Service Area — Strong SEO Potential

You serve 20 cities across Baldwin and Mobile counties. Each city is a keyword opportunity: “plumber Robertsdale,” “plumber Foley,” “plumber Daphne,” “plumber Mobile.” A modern website with dedicated city pages could capture search traffic across your entire service area — right now that potential is completely untapped.

Facebook Pixel Installed — You’ve Advertised Before

Having a Facebook Pixel means you’ve run social media ads at some point. This shows you understand the value of digital marketing. A modernized website would make future ad campaigns much more effective by converting clicks into calls.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Rebuild the Website on a Modern Platform

Highest Priority

Replace the 2018 GoDaddy template with a clean, mobile-first design. Include your phone number prominently at the top of every page, an emergency service button, your full list of services, and a contact form. Add proper meta descriptions, JSON-LD PlumberService schema, and update to Google Analytics 4. The new site should look like it was built this year — because it will be.

2. Create City-Specific Service Pages

Highest Priority

Build a separate page for each major city you serve: Robertsdale, Foley, Daphne, Fairhope, Gulf Shores, Spanish Fort, Mobile. Each page should target searches like “plumber [city] AL” with content specific to that area. This strategy alone could put you on page one for multiple cities simultaneously.

3. Optimize Google Business Profile & Collect Reviews

Medium Priority

Make sure your Google Business Profile is fully optimized with photos, services, and your updated website URL. Start actively asking clients for Google reviews after every job. For a plumber, reviews are the #1 factor in getting the call. Go from wherever you are now to 20+ reviews and you’ll see a measurable increase in phone calls.
