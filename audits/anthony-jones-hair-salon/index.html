Prioritized Recommendations

1. Consolidate to a Single H1 Tag High Impact

What's wrong: The homepage has 5 H1 tags, confusing search engines about the page's primary topic.
Why it matters: Multiple H1s dilute keyword relevance and make it harder for Google to determine what the page is about.
How to fix: Keep one H1 (e.g., "Anthony Jones Hair Salon — Orange Beach Since 1967") and change the rest to H2 or H3 tags.
Estimated impact: Could improve ranking for primary keywords by 10-20 positions.

2. 301 Redirect Legacy Domain High Impact

What's wrong: anthonyjonesinc.net still exists separately, splitting backlinks and brand authority between two domains.
Why it matters: Google treats these as separate sites — any links or authority built on the old domain aren't helping the new one.
How to fix: Set up a permanent 301 redirect from anthonyjonesinc.net to anthonyjoneshairsalon.com for all pages.
Estimated impact: Consolidates all link equity into one domain — potentially significant ranking boost.

3. Add Alt Text to All Images Medium Impact

What's wrong: Images throughout the site are missing descriptive alt text.
Why it matters: Alt text helps Google understand image content and drives traffic from Google Image Search — especially valuable for salons.
How to fix: Add descriptive alt text to every image (e.g., "balayage hair coloring at Anthony Jones Hair Salon Orange Beach").
Estimated impact: Opens up Google Image Search traffic and improves accessibility compliance.

4. Add LocalBusiness Schema Markup Medium Impact

What's wrong: No structured data helps Google understand this is a local hair salon business.
Why it matters: Schema markup enables rich results in Google (hours, reviews, location) which dramatically increase click-through rates.
How to fix: Add HairSalon or BeautySalon schema with name, address, phone, hours, geo-coordinates, and aggregate rating.
Estimated impact: Rich results can increase CTR by 20-30%.

5. Leverage 57-Year History in SEO Strategy Moderate Impact

What's wrong: The incredible 57-year history is underutilized for SEO differentiation.
Why it matters: Longevity builds trust with both Google (E-E-A-T) and potential customers — no competitor can match this.
How to fix: Create an "Our Story" page targeting "oldest hair salon Orange Beach," add founding year to title tags, and build content around the salon's history.
Estimated impact: Unique content angle that no competitor can replicate — strong differentiation.
