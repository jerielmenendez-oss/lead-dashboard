Executive Summary

B2 Painting & Home Improvement is doing more online than most contractors in the area — you have a website, active social media on Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter, a Yelp listing with 32 photos, and a BBB A rating. You’re even running Google Ads. That shows you understand the value of being online.

The problem is execution. Your website is built on a free platform (Neo) with a .co.site domain that screams “I didn’t invest in this.” You have a second domain (b2painters.com) splitting your online presence in two. Your title tag is truncated (“Home Imp.”), every image on the site is missing alt text, there’s no meta description, no business schema markup, and your hours differ between your website and Yelp. You’re paying for Google Ads to send traffic to a site that Google barely trusts. A proper website on your own domain would make your ad spend work 3–5x harder.

Key Findings

Free Platform Domain (.co.site) Kills Credibility

Your website is on b2painting.co.site — a free subdomain from Neo, an AI website builder. This is like having a business card printed on a napkin. Homeowners spending thousands on a paint job want to see a professional web presence. A .co.site domain signals that the business didn’t invest in a real website, which makes customers question whether you’ll invest in their project.

Found at: b2painting.co.site

Two Websites Splitting Your Online Presence

You have both b2painting.co.site and b2painters.com. Having two separate websites is worse than having one — it splits your search authority between two domains, confuses Google, and makes it harder for either one to rank. Pick one domain and redirect the other to it.

Found at: b2painters.com (second domain)

No Meta Description on Any Page

Your website has no meta description — that’s the short text preview that shows up in Google search results under your title. Without it, Google pulls random text from your page, which usually looks messy and doesn’t convince anyone to click. Every page needs a clear, compelling description.

Every Image Missing Alt Text

Your logo, all service photos (residential, commercial, drywall, interior, exterior), all 6 portfolio photos, and testimonial images have no alt text. This means Google can’t “see” your images, they don’t show up in Google Image search, and your site is less accessible to visually impaired visitors. For a painting company, image search traffic is a major missed opportunity.

Title Tag Truncated — Looks Unprofessional

Your page title shows as “Painting Services Foley AL | B2 Painting & Home Imp.” — the name is cut off. This is what shows in Google results and browser tabs. It makes the business look like it didn’t bother to check how it appears online.

No Local Business Schema Markup

Your site only has a basic “WebSite” schema. It’s missing LocalBusiness, PaintingContractor, service area, hours, reviews, and location data that Google uses to understand and display your business in search results. Adding this structured data helps Google show your business info (hours, phone, rating) directly in search results.

Hours Mismatch: Website vs. Yelp

Your website says Monday–Friday 7am–5pm. Your Yelp listing says Monday–Saturday 7am–5pm. When your hours don’t match across platforms, Google trusts your business information less, and customers get confused about when you’re actually available.

Birmingham Phone Number in Foley

Your phone number (205) is a Birmingham area code, but your business is in Foley. Local customers may hesitate to call what looks like an out-of-area number. Consider getting a local (251) number or at least make your Foley address prominent so customers know you’re local.

Active on Multiple Social Platforms

You have Instagram (@bandbb2painting, 28 posts), Facebook, and Twitter accounts. For a painting company, Instagram is especially valuable — before-and-after photos sell painting services better than anything. Keep posting project photos regularly.

Yelp Listing with 32 Photos

Your Yelp listing has 32 photos — that’s excellent for social proof. Yelp is one of the top platforms customers use to vet contractors. This is working in your favor.

BBB A Rating & Running Google Ads

Your BBB A rating adds credibility. And the fact that you’re running Google Ads shows you’re already investing in lead generation. A professional website would make every dollar you spend on ads work significantly harder by converting more clicks into actual calls.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Move to a Professional Domain & Consolidate

Highest Priority

Register a proper domain like b2painting.com (or use your existing b2painters.com). Build a professional website there and redirect the other domain to it. Stop splitting your online presence. A real domain with proper hosting will rank better, convert more visitors, and make your Google Ads work harder. Your current site on .co.site is actively hurting your credibility and your ad spend ROI.

2. Fix All Missing SEO Elements

Highest Priority

Add meta descriptions to every page. Add alt text to every image (describe what’s in the photo: “interior house painting Foley AL living room”). Fix the truncated title tag. Add LocalBusiness and PaintingContractor schema markup with your address, phone, hours, and service area. These are foundational SEO elements that every competitor already has.

3. Fix Business Info Consistency

Medium Priority

Make your hours match everywhere (website says Mon–Fri, Yelp says Mon–Sat — pick one and update all platforms). Verify your Google Business Profile is fully optimized with consistent name, address, and phone number across every listing. This consistency is a major ranking factor for local search.
