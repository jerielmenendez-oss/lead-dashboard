Overall Digital Presence Score: 19 / 100

Bagel Boy is a growing multi-location business with a loyal customer base and strong Google ratings — but it has no website, no Instagram presence, and no way for new customers to browse the menu before visiting. The digital infrastructure has not kept pace with the business's growth.

Executive Summary

With two locations serving the Gulf Coast — Foley and Orange Beach — Bagel Boy has built real momentum. A 4.5-star rating across 46 Google reviews is evidence that customers genuinely love this place. But growing from one location to two demands more than word of mouth. Right now, a tourist searching for bagels in Orange Beach, or a local in Foley looking for breakfast options, has no website to land on, no Instagram to scroll, and no online menu to browse. That gap is costing Bagel Boy customers every single day. The foundation is strong — now it's time to build the digital side of the business to match.

Key Findings

No Website for Either Location

Neither the Foley nor Orange Beach location has a website. Customers looking to check hours, find the address, or view a menu before driving over have no online resource. For a business serving a tourist-heavy area like Orange Beach, this is a significant missed opportunity — visitors plan trips online and choose restaurants that are easy to find.

No Instagram Presence

Bagels are a highly visual, photogenic product. Instagram is where food businesses — especially breakfast spots — build loyal followings and attract new customers. Having no Instagram means missing out on an entire channel of organic discovery, especially among younger customers and tourists who browse food content daily.

Facebook is the Only Digital Platform

Relying on a single platform means all of your digital customer communication is dependent on one company's algorithms and policies. A single account issue or algorithm shift can dramatically reduce your reach overnight. Diversifying platforms protects your audience and expands your discovery.

No Online Menu Available

Customers increasingly decide where to eat before leaving home. Without a menu accessible online — whether on a website or a platform like Google Business Profile — potential customers cannot confirm you have what they want and may choose a competitor with a visible menu instead.

Strong Google Reviews — 4.5 Stars, 46 Reviews

A 4.5-star average with nearly 50 reviews shows genuine customer satisfaction. This is a solid foundation that new digital marketing efforts can build upon. Positive reviews also directly boost Google Maps ranking for local searches.

Recommendations

1. Build a Multi-Location Website

High Priority

Create a single website that covers both locations — with separate pages or sections for Foley and Orange Beach, each listing their address, hours, and phone number. Include a full menu, high-quality food photos, and a short "About" section. This becomes your 24/7 salesperson and gives Google the content it needs to rank you in local search results.

2. Create an Instagram Account

High Priority

Start an Instagram profile for Bagel Boy and post photos of your most popular bagels and dishes regularly. Bagel content performs exceptionally well on Instagram — colorful spreads, stacked toppings, and morning coffee aesthetics are proven crowd-pleasers. This is a free channel that can drive meaningful foot traffic, especially from tourists in the Orange Beach area.

3. Claim and Optimize Google Business Profiles for Both Locations

High Priority

Ensure both the Foley and Orange Beach locations have fully claimed, verified Google Business Profiles with accurate hours, phone numbers, menus, and photos. Verified profiles with complete information rank significantly higher in Google Maps. Tourists searching "bagels near me" in Orange Beach need to find you immediately.

4. Upload Your Menu to Google Business Profile

Medium Priority

Google allows businesses to upload menus directly to their GBP listing. This is a quick win — customers can see your offerings without leaving Google, which increases the likelihood they choose you over a competitor. Do this for both locations as soon as profiles are verified.

5. Ask Happy Customers for Google Reviews

Medium Priority

Your 4.5-star average is great, but 46 reviews across two locations means each location averages around 23 reviews — which is relatively low. Place a small sign at the register or print a note on receipts asking satisfied customers to leave a Google review. More reviews increase your visibility and trustworthiness in search results.
