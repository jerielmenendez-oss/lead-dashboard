Executive Summary

Carruth Construction has been building in Foley since 2001. That’s 25 years of experience in fences, decks, roofing, painting, siding, and marine construction — a wide range of services that should be generating steady leads online.

Instead, you are one of the most invisible businesses we’ve audited. There is no website, no phone number available online, no social media, no email address, and no way for a new customer to contact you without already knowing someone who has your number. Your only online trace is a Yelp listing. In a market where homeowners start every contractor search on Google, this means you’re relying 100% on word-of-mouth — which has worked for 25 years, but is leaving a significant amount of business on the table.

Key Findings

No Website At All

Carruth Construction has no website of any kind. When a homeowner in Foley needs a fence, deck, or roof repair and searches Google, they find competitors like Edgewater Marine, Titan Marine Construction, H.E. Roberts Roofing, and Platinum Roofing — all with professional websites. You simply don’t appear.

No Phone Number Publicly Available

We could not find a phone number for Carruth Construction anywhere online. This is the most basic piece of business information, and without it, even motivated customers who find your Yelp listing cannot call you. This is almost certainly costing you jobs every week.

No Google Business Profile

There is no Google Business Profile for Carruth Construction. This means you don’t appear in Google Maps or the local “map pack” that shows up at the top of search results. When someone searches “fence builder Foley AL,” the map shows your competitors — never you.

No Social Media Presence

No Facebook, no Instagram, no social media of any kind. For a business that builds fences, decks, and does marine construction, before-and-after photos are your most powerful marketing tool. You’re not using any platform to showcase your work.

Wide Service Range — Wasted Without a Website

Fences, decks, roofing, painting, siding, and marine construction — that’s at least 6 different service categories you could rank for in Google. Each one is a keyword opportunity: “fence builder Foley,” “deck builder Baldwin County,” “marine construction Foley.” Without a website, you’re invisible for all of them.

Yelp Has an Unanswered Negative Review

Your Yelp listing has at least one negative review that you haven’t responded to. Because your Yelp listing is one of the only things people find when searching for you, this unanswered review is shaping how potential customers see your business. Claiming your Yelp listing and responding professionally would show future customers you care about their experience.

25 Years in Business — Incredible Longevity

A quarter century in the construction business is a powerful trust signal. Homeowners want to hire contractors who will still be around if they need warranty work. “Serving Foley since 2001” on a website header would instantly set you apart from newer competitors.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Get a Phone Number Online & Claim Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Right now, nobody can call you because nobody can find your number. Step one is getting a Google Business Profile at business.google.com with your phone number, address, services, and hours. This is free and will immediately make you findable on Google Maps. Add photos of your best fence, deck, and roofing work.

2. Build a Professional Website with Service Pages

Highest Priority

Create a website with separate pages for each service: fences, decks, roofing, painting, siding, and marine construction. Each page should have photos of completed work, a description of what you do, and your phone number. This gives Google 6+ pages to index, meaning you can show up for 6+ different types of searches. Include your 25-year history prominently.

3. Create a Facebook Page & Post Project Photos

Medium Priority

A Facebook business page is free and takes minutes to set up. Start posting before-and-after photos of your projects. Tag your location on every post. In construction, people hire with their eyes — showing what you’ve built is the most effective marketing there is.
