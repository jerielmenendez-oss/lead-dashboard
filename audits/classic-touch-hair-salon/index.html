Prioritized Recommendations

1. Fix to Single H1 Tag High Impact

What's wrong: The homepage has 4 H1 tags, confusing Google about the page's primary topic.
Why it matters: Multiple H1s dilute the relevance signal for your most important keywords. Google doesn't know which heading to prioritize.
How to fix: Keep one H1 (e.g., "Classic Touch Hair Salon — Gulf Shores, Alabama") and demote the rest to H2 or H3 tags.
Estimated impact: Strengthens relevance signals for "hair salon Gulf Shores" — your money keyword.

2. Fix All HTTP Links to HTTPS High Impact

What's wrong: Some internal links and resources still reference HTTP instead of HTTPS, creating mixed content.
Why it matters: Mixed content triggers browser security warnings, erodes visitor trust, and is a negative ranking signal for Google.
How to fix: Search and replace all http:// references to https:// in the WordPress database. Use a plugin like Better Search Replace or Really Simple SSL.
Estimated impact: Eliminates security warnings and strengthens HTTPS ranking signal.

3. Add Online Booking System Medium Impact

What's wrong: Customers can only book by phone — no online scheduling option.
Why it matters: Modern consumers expect online booking. Phone-only booking loses customers who search after hours or prefer not to call.
How to fix: Integrate a booking platform like Vagaro, Square Appointments, or Booksy. Add a prominent "Book Now" button.
Estimated impact: Can increase bookings by 20-40%, especially from younger demographics and after-hours searchers.

4. Add LocalBusiness Schema Markup Medium Impact

What's wrong: No structured data of any kind exists on the site.
Why it matters: Schema markup enables rich results in Google — showing hours, reviews, phone number, and more directly in search results.
How to fix: Add HairSalon schema with name, address, phone, hours, geo-coordinates, services, and aggregate rating.
Estimated impact: Rich results can increase click-through rates by 20-30%.

5. Add Image Alt Text Medium Impact

What's wrong: Images across the site have no alt text attributes.
Why it matters: Alt text helps Google understand images and drives traffic from Google Image Search — people searching for hairstyle inspiration.
How to fix: Add descriptive alt text to every image (e.g., "balayage highlights at Classic Touch Hair Salon Gulf Shores").
Estimated impact: Opens up Google Image Search traffic — particularly valuable for hair salons.

6. Capitalize on Domain Name with Content Strategy Moderate Impact

What's wrong: hairgulfshores.com is an incredible exact-match keyword domain, but there's minimal content leveraging it.
Why it matters: This domain gives a natural ranking advantage for "hair Gulf Shores" queries. With supporting content, it could dominate local search.
How to fix: Create service pages targeting specific keywords: "hair coloring Gulf Shores," "haircuts Gulf Shores," "bridal hair Gulf Shores." Add a blog with local content.
Estimated impact: Combined with the domain advantage, a content strategy could make this the top-ranking salon in Gulf Shores.
