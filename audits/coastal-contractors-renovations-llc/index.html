Executive Summary

Coastal Contractors & Renovations handles commercial construction and condo renovations in Orange Beach — a market that demands professionalism and attention to detail. Property managers and condo associations will research your business online before awarding a contract.

Your WordPress/Avada website has serious issues that undermine your credibility. A broken navigation link (“acility-maintenance” instead of “Facility Maintenance”) makes the site look careless. The embedded Facebook feed is likely broken. Multiple competing calls-to-action confuse visitors instead of guiding them. Your domain name (coastalcontractorsandrenovations.com) is also extremely long — hard to type, share verbally, or fit on a business card.

Key Findings

Broken Navigation Link — “acility-maintenance”

Your navigation menu has a typo: it says “acility-maintenance” instead of “Facility Maintenance.” This is the first thing a property manager sees when visiting your site. For a commercial contractor handling six-figure renovation projects, a typo in the navigation is a red flag about attention to detail.

Found: Navigation link text shows "acility-maintenance"

Broken Facebook Feed

The Facebook feed embedded on your site is likely not loading. Broken social widgets create visual gaps and make the site look neglected. Either fix the integration or remove it entirely — a broken widget is worse than no widget.

Cluttered Calls-to-Action

Your site has too many competing CTAs (calls-to-action) fighting for attention. When everything is a call-to-action, nothing is. Simplify to one primary CTA per page: “Request a Quote” or “Schedule a Consultation.”

Extremely Long Domain Name

coastalcontractorsandrenovations.com is 38 characters. That’s nearly impossible to share verbally, type correctly, or fit on marketing materials. A shorter domain (like coastalcontractorsob.com or ccr-construction.com) would be much more practical.

Not BBB Accredited

For a commercial contractor working with property managers and condo associations, BBB accreditation is often expected. Not being accredited may disqualify you from consideration before you even get a chance to bid.

Commercial Focus — Higher-Value Projects

Commercial construction and condo renovations are higher-value projects with longer relationships. A polished web presence tailored to property managers and HOA boards would help you win more of these contracts.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Fix the Broken Navigation and Clean Up the Site

Highest Priority

Fix the “acility-maintenance” typo, remove or fix the broken Facebook feed, and simplify your CTAs to one clear action per page. These are quick fixes that immediately improve your credibility with property managers. Consider a full site redesign on a cleaner theme.

2. Create a Commercial-Focused Portfolio

Highest Priority

Property managers want to see completed commercial projects similar to theirs. Create a portfolio page showing condo renovations, facility maintenance work, and commercial builds. Include the scope of work, timeline, and location for each project. This is what wins commercial contracts.

3. Get a Shorter Domain & Pursue BBB Accreditation

Medium Priority

Register a shorter, more memorable domain and redirect the long one to it. Apply for BBB accreditation — for commercial work, the BBB badge is a trust signal that property managers actively look for.
