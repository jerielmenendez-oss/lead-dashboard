Executive Summary

Coastal Fitness has a well-built WordPress website that already does many things right — a strong title tag with location keywords, a solid meta description, and compelling conversion elements like before/after sliders and testimonials. The main areas for improvement are technical: the H1 tag is focused on a form CTA rather than keywords, there's no LocalBusiness schema markup, and heavy JavaScript may be slowing page load times. Addressing these relatively straightforward issues could push this site from good to excellent in local search performance.

Prioritized Recommendations

1. Add a Keyword-Rich H1 Tag

High ImpactPriority: Immediate
What's Wrong
The H1 tag is "Fill out the form for a FREE INTRO SESSION" — conversion-focused but not SEO-optimized.
Why It Matters
The H1 is one of Google's strongest on-page signals. A keyword-rich H1 improves rankings for target terms while the form CTA can live as a separate element.
How to Fix
Add a prominent H1 like "Foley's Premier Gym — Personal Training, CrossFit & Group Fitness" at the top of the page. Keep the form CTA as a styled div or H2.
Estimated Impact
Improved rankings for "gym Foley AL" and related terms within 4-6 weeks.

2. Add LocalBusiness Schema Markup

High ImpactPriority: Immediate
What's Wrong
No structured data despite having all the necessary business information on the site.
Why It Matters
Schema markup helps Google display rich results with hours, ratings, price range, and services directly in search results.
How to Fix
Add JSON-LD HealthClub/ExerciseGym schema to the WordPress header with business name, address (Foley, AL), phone ((251) 747-2687), hours, owners (Amber and Carlos Lizarraga), and service offerings.
Estimated Impact
Enhanced search appearance and 10-15% improvement in click-through rates.

3. Optimize JavaScript Loading

Medium ImpactPriority: This Week
What's Wrong
Heavy page builder JavaScript may slow initial page load, especially on mobile connections.
Why It Matters
Page speed is a confirmed Google ranking factor. Slow sites also see higher bounce rates — every extra second costs ~7% in conversions.
How to Fix
Defer non-critical JS, lazy-load below-the-fold images and sliders, and consider a caching plugin like WP Rocket if not already in use alongside Cloudflare.
Estimated Impact
Faster load times, lower bounce rate, and incremental ranking improvement.

4. Add an FAQ Section for Local Searches

Medium ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
No FAQ section exists to capture question-based searches like "best gym in Foley" or "CrossFit vs personal training."
Why It Matters
FAQ pages with FAQPage schema can earn featured snippets in Google, dramatically increasing visibility. They also address objections that prevent conversions.
How to Fix
Add an FAQ section with 8-10 common questions about membership, classes, pricing, and fitness goals. Mark up with FAQPage schema.
Estimated Impact
Potential featured snippet appearances and new long-tail keyword rankings.

5. Add Structured Data for Classes/Events

Low ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
Class schedules and events are not marked up with structured data.
Why It Matters
Event and class schema can make your schedule appear directly in Google results, driving more qualified traffic.
How to Fix
Add Event schema for recurring classes with name, time, location, and description.
Estimated Impact
Enhanced visibility for class-specific searches.
