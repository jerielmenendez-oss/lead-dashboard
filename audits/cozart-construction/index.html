Executive Summary

Cozart Construction is a young, growing business with real momentum — 4.5 stars, a BBB A+ rating, a top-12% BuildZoom score, and listings on HomeAdvisor and Birdeye. That’s a strong foundation for a company that’s only been around since 2020.

Key Findings

No Website — Facebook Page Only

Cozart Construction has no website. Your Facebook page is listed as your “website” on directory sites, but Facebook pages don’t rank well in Google search. When a homeowner searches “remodeling contractor Foley AL,” they find competitors with real websites — not Facebook pages.

Three Different Phone Numbers Online

We found three different phone numbers listed for your business: (251) 310-3311, (251) 223-0665, and (251) 229-2360. When Google sees conflicting information, it trusts your listings less and ranks you lower. Customers who call the wrong number may think you’re out of business. Pick one number and make it the same everywhere.

Two Different Addresses Listed

Some directories show 7470 Brompton Drive in Foley, while others show 606 Shepard St in Gulf Shores. Like the phone number issue, this inconsistency hurts your credibility with both Google and customers.

License May Need Renewal

BuildZoom shows your Alabama Home Builders License (#28363) with a December 2025 expiration. If this hasn’t been renewed yet, it needs to be — and the updated info should be reflected across all your online listings.

4.5 Stars & BuildZoom Top 12%

Your 4.5-star rating on Birdeye (8 reviews) and BuildZoom score of 101 (top 12% in Alabama) are excellent for a young company. These are trust signals that should be front and center on a website — right now they’re buried on third-party sites most customers will never visit.

BBB A+ Rating

Your Better Business Bureau A+ rating is a strong credibility marker, especially for a construction company. Homeowners actively check BBB before hiring contractors. This should be prominently displayed on your website once you have one.

Listed on HomeAdvisor

Having a HomeAdvisor listing is good — it’s one of the top platforms homeowners use to find contractors. But without your own website to link back to, you’re paying for leads on their platform instead of generating your own.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Build a Professional Website

Highest Priority

You need a clean, mobile-friendly website that shows your services, service area, project photos, your BBB A+ badge, and your BuildZoom ranking. Include a contact form and your phone number on every page. This is the single most impactful thing you can do to grow — it turns Google searches into phone calls.

2. Fix Your Business Information Everywhere

Highest Priority

Pick one phone number and one address, then update every listing — BBB, BuildZoom, HomeAdvisor, Birdeye, Facebook, and your future Google Business Profile. Right now, the conflicting info is actively hurting you. Google ranks businesses with consistent information higher than those with mismatches.

3. Claim & Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Go to business.google.com and claim your listing (or create one if it doesn’t exist). Add your services, photos of completed work, your hours, and a description highlighting your BBB A+ rating and top-12% BuildZoom score. Then start asking happy customers to leave Google reviews. Even getting to 15–20 reviews would put you ahead of most competitors in Foley.
