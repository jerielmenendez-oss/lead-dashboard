Detailed Findings

Website Quality (7/25)

Has a Custom Domain

The domain mycutterscorner.com is a solid asset. HTTPS is enabled. However, the Thryv/LiRP template limits customization and looks identical to thousands of other businesses.

Generic Template — Zero Differentiation

The layout, design, and structure are cookie-cutter. Nothing about the design says "Cutter's Corner." A generic template signals "we didn't invest in our image" to potential clients.

SEO Fundamentals (3/20)

Template-Generated SEO Elements

Title tags and meta descriptions are likely generic and auto-generated by Thryv. No HairSalon or LocalBusiness schema markup. No geo-targeted content for "Foley AL" searches. Platform restricts page creation.

Google Business Profile (7/20)

Directory Links Exist

The site links to Google, Yelp, and Facebook — a positive foundation. NAP: 7857 Highway 59, Foley AL 36535. Business hours listed (Mon-Tue 9-3, Wed-Fri 9-5, Sat 9-2). But the website itself provides no local SEO value.

Social Media (5/15)

Facebook Active, Instagram Missing

Facebook page exists and is linked. However, there is no Instagram presence — a critical gap for a hair salon in 2026. Instagram is the #1 platform for showcasing salon work.

User Experience (4/10)

No Real Online Booking

The booking CTA just triggers a phone call to (251) 987-0999. No actual online scheduling system. After-hours visitors and younger demographics who expect to book online are lost to competitors.

Gmail Email — Unprofessional

Using gabby.cutterscorner@gmail.com instead of gabby@mycutterscorner.com. A Gmail address looks amateurish and wastes a branding opportunity.

Online Reputation (4/10)

No Gallery, Testimonials, or Reviews on Site

No before/after gallery, no stylist profiles, no client testimonials. A hair salon lives and dies by visual proof of its work — potential clients can't see the quality.

Prioritized Recommendations

1. Create an Instagram Business Profile

High ImpactPriority: Immediate
What's Wrong
No Instagram presence at all. This is a critical gap for a hair salon.
Why It Matters
Instagram is the #1 platform for showcasing salon work. Before/after photos and transformation reels drive massive engagement and new client bookings.
How to Fix
Create an Instagram business profile and start posting before/after photos immediately. Short transformation reels perform extremely well for salons.
Estimated Impact
New client discovery channel and visual portfolio that builds trust before booking.

2. Set Up Professional Email & Real Online Booking

High ImpactPriority: Immediate
What's Wrong
Using Gmail instead of domain email. Booking CTA just triggers a phone call — no actual online scheduling.
Why It Matters
A Gmail address reduces trust. Phone-only booking loses after-hours visitors and younger demographics who expect to book online.
How to Fix
Set up gabby@mycutterscorner.com via Google Workspace. Implement a booking system (Square Appointments, Vagaro, or Booksy) that allows clients to pick a stylist, service, and time slot online.
Estimated Impact
Professional image and 24/7 booking capability capturing clients competitors miss.

3. Build a Custom Website

High ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
The Thryv/LiRP template is generic, provides almost no SEO value, and looks identical to thousands of other businesses.
Why It Matters
Template platforms give the illusion of having a website while providing almost no SEO value. Competitors with custom sites rank higher and convert better.
How to Fix
Build a custom website with unique branding, a before/after gallery, stylist profiles with photos and specialties, and integrated booking.
Estimated Impact
Professional first impression, SEO foundation, and higher visitor-to-client conversion.

4. Create a Before/After Gallery & Stylist Profiles

High ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
No gallery showcasing work. No stylist profiles. No client testimonials on the site.
Why It Matters
A before/after gallery is the single best conversion tool for a salon. Potential clients need visual proof of quality before they book.
How to Fix
Create a gallery page with transformation photos. Add stylist profiles with photos, specialties, and individual booking links. Pull client testimonials from Google and Yelp reviews.
Estimated Impact
Significantly higher conversion rate from website visitors to booked appointments.

5. Add HairSalon + LocalBusiness Schema Markup

Medium ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
No structured data markup on the site.
Why It Matters
Schema markup helps Google display rich results with hours, ratings, and services directly in search results.
How to Fix
Add JSON-LD HairSalon/LocalBusiness schema to the new website with business name, address, phone, hours, and services.
Estimated Impact
Enhanced search appearance and improved click-through rates.

6. Set Up Google Review Generation

Medium ImpactPriority: This Month
What's Wrong
No system for generating or displaying reviews on the website.
Why It Matters
Google reviews are one of the top local ranking factors and the #1 trust signal for new clients choosing a salon.
How to Fix
Set up post-appointment review follow-ups. Add a reviews widget to the homepage. Use QR codes at the register linking to the Google review page.
Estimated Impact
Improved GBP ranking and stronger social proof driving bookings.

7. Build Citations & Optimize for Local Keywords

Low ImpactPriority: Month 2-3
What's Wrong
No local keyword optimization. Citation consistency needs verification across directories.
Why It Matters
Consistent NAP across all directories strengthens local SEO. Keyword-optimized content ranks for searches clients are actually making.
How to Fix
Build consistent local citations on Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, and salon directories. Optimize for "hair salon Foley AL," "haircut near me," and "women's haircut Foley." Consider extended hours or adjust CTAs to capture after-hours interest via online booking.
Estimated Impact
Broader local visibility and ongoing organic traffic growth.
