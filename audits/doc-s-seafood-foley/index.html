Executive Summary

Doc's Seafood Foley is running on WordPress with the Yoast SEO plugin installed, which provides a strong technical foundation — but critical basics are being missed. The homepage lacks an H1 tag entirely and has no meta description tag in the HTML head (only within schema markup), meaning Google has to guess what this page is about. The restaurant menus are locked inside PDF files that Google cannot easily index and that frustrate mobile users. With these foundational issues addressed, Doc's could see significant improvement in local search visibility for Foley seafood restaurant queries.

Prioritized Recommendations

1. Add an H1 Tag to the Homepage High Impact

What's wrong: The homepage has no H1 tag at all — Google doesn't know what this page is about.
Why it matters: The H1 is one of the strongest on-page SEO signals. Without it, you're invisible for your most important keywords.
How to fix: Add an H1 tag like "Doc's Seafood — Fresh Seafood Restaurant in Foley, Alabama" to the homepage.
Estimated impact: Could immediately improve rankings for "seafood restaurant Foley AL" and related terms.

2. Add a Meta Description Tag High Impact

What's wrong: No meta description tag exists in the page HTML — Yoast is installed but not configured for this.
Why it matters: The meta description controls what people see in Google search results. Without one, Google generates a random snippet.
How to fix: In Yoast SEO settings, add a compelling description: "Fresh Gulf seafood in Foley, AL. Family-friendly dining with daily catches, shrimp, oysters & more. Visit Doc's Seafood today!"
Estimated impact: Can increase click-through rate from search results by 15-25%.

3. Convert PDF Menus to HTML Pages High Impact

What's wrong: Restaurant menus are PDF downloads — Google can barely read them and mobile users hate them.
Why it matters: Menu items are some of the most searched terms for restaurants. PDF content is nearly invisible to search engines.
How to fix: Create dedicated HTML menu pages with proper headings, descriptions, and schema markup for each menu section.
Estimated impact: Opens up dozens of long-tail keyword opportunities ("fried shrimp Foley AL", "oysters near me").

4. Add Online Ordering Medium Impact

What's wrong: No way to order food online from the website.
Why it matters: Online ordering is expected by consumers and reduces friction. It also generates direct revenue vs. third-party delivery apps taking 20-30%.
How to fix: Integrate a platform like Square Online, Toast, or ChowNow directly into the WordPress site.
Estimated impact: Can increase direct orders and reduce third-party delivery commissions.

5. Expand Homepage Content Medium Impact

What's wrong: The homepage has very little text content for Google to index.
Why it matters: Google needs substantial, relevant text to understand what a page is about and rank it appropriately.
How to fix: Add sections for: restaurant story, featured dishes, location/hours, customer reviews, and a brief menu overview with links to full menus.
Estimated impact: More indexable content = more keyword opportunities and stronger topical relevance.
