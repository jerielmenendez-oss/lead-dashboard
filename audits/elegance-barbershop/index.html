Detailed Findings

Website Quality (4/25)

No Real Website — Using hub-24 Directory Page

The primary web presence is on a hub-24 subdomain — a basic directory template with limited customization, no custom domain, and minimal control over performance, design, or functionality.

Brand Fragmentation Across 3 Platforms

The business has a hub-24 directory page, a separate WordPress blog, and social media profiles all operating independently. Google doesn't know which page to rank, and link equity is split instead of building authority on one domain.

SEO Fundamentals (2/20)

No Custom Domain

Using elegancebarbershopfoley.hub-24.com — a subdomain on someone else's platform. Google treats hub-24.com as the domain, not Elegance Barbershop. The URL is unprofessional and hard to remember.

Zero SEO Control

Title tags, meta descriptions, and URL structure are all controlled by the hub-24 template. No schema markup, no custom URLs, and the disconnected WordPress blog adds confusion rather than value.

Google Business Profile (7/20)

GBP Exists but Undermined by Fragmentation

Google Business Profile is active and visible on Maps, but NAP information (209 W 9th Ave, Foley AL 36535) is spread across three platforms with potentially conflicting data. Multiple URLs create citation confusion.

Social Media (8/15)

Strong Instagram Engagement

Instagram is the standout asset with good engagement. Facebook page is also active. The social presence proves customers care about this shop — it just needs a proper website to direct that energy to.

User Experience (3/10)

No Online Booking

There's no way to book appointments online from any of the three platforms. A barbershop without online booking in 2026 is losing walk-up digital traffic to competitors.

No shop story, no barber profiles, minimal photos, and no pricing information. The Latin barbershop identity — a real differentiator — is not being showcased.
