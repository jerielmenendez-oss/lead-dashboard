01. Executive Summary

Gulf Coast Auto Repair has a website — but it's stuck in the early 2000s and is actively hurting the business. The site at gulfcoastautorepair.net has no SSL certificate (no HTTPS), uses table-based layout, displays stock photos instead of real images, shows the business as "Closed," has no contact form, and no social media presence. Modern browsers display security warnings for HTTP-only sites, causing visitors to leave immediately. A complete rebuild is urgently needed.

02. Detailed Findings

Website Performance & Security

No HTTPS — Security Warning Displayed

The website runs on HTTP without an SSL certificate. Chrome, Firefox, and Safari all display "Not Secure" warnings to visitors. Google also penalizes non-HTTPS sites in search rankings. This is a critical trust and SEO issue.

Critical
2000s-Era Table Layout — Not Mobile Responsive

The site uses HTML table-based layout, a technique abandoned nearly 20 years ago. It does not display correctly on mobile devices. With 60%+ of local searches happening on mobile, this means most visitors have a broken experience.

15 / 15
Stock Photos — No Authentic Imagery

The website uses generic stock photos instead of real photos of the shop, staff, or actual work. This destroys trust and authenticity — customers want to see the real business before visiting.

03. Prioritized Recommendations

  1. Complete website rebuild (urgent) — Replace the entire site with a modern, mobile-responsive design. Include HTTPS, real photos, service pages, online quote request form, and clear hours/location. The current site is actively losing customers.
  2. Fix the "Closed" status immediately — If the business is open, this must be corrected on the website and Google Business Profile right away. Every day this shows "Closed" is lost revenue.
  3. Install SSL certificate (HTTPS) — This is required for Google ranking and customer trust. If rebuilding, this will be included. If patching, do this first.
  4. Replace stock photos with real images — Professional photos of the shop, team, equipment, and completed work. Authenticity builds trust in the auto repair industry.
  5. Optimize Google Business Profile — Ensure hours are correct, add professional photos, complete service list, and actively manage reviews.
  6. Add online quote/contact form — Let customers describe their vehicle issue and request a callback or estimate online.
  7. Create a Facebook page — Share shop updates, customer stories, maintenance tips, and seasonal promotions. Build community trust.
  8. Implement local SEO strategy — Target "auto repair Foley AL," "mechanic near me," "brake repair Foley," and similar high-intent keywords.
