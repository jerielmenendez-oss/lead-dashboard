Detailed Findings

Website Quality (2/25)

Severely Outdated Website — Actively Losing Customers

The website is built on Homestead, a website builder that was discontinued years ago. It uses table-based HTML layout from the early 2000s. It is not responsive — it breaks on phones and tablets. There is no HTTPS, so browsers display a "Not Secure" warning. The site displays a "Closed" status. Every element signals "this business is outdated or abandoned."

SEO Fundamentals (1/20)

No Semantic HTML or Modern SEO

The table-based layout means there is no semantic HTML structure. No proper headings, no schema markup, no sitemap. The site uses legacy HTML that search engines struggle to parse correctly. Google cannot properly understand or rank content in table-based layouts.

Content Quality

Stock Photos and No Trust Signals

Stock photos signal inauthenticity. No real photos of the shop, staff, or work. No contact form means the only way to reach the business is by phone. No service detail or trust signals. Auto repair is a trust-dependent industry — customers need to see real photos, real reviews, and detailed service descriptions before trusting a shop with their vehicle.

Google Business Profile (3/20)

"Closed" Status — Catastrophic Issue

The "Closed" status is catastrophic — it may be reflected on GBP as well. The .net domain is less trusted than .com. NAP consistency is likely fragmented across old and new listings. If Google shows this business as closed, it is effectively delisted from local search.

Social Media (0/15)

Zero Social Media Presence

Zero social media presence. No Facebook, no Instagram, nothing. No way for customers to engage, see recent work, or verify the business is active. For a business currently showing as "Closed," social proof of activity is essential.

User Experience (1/10)

Broken Mobile Experience and No Contact Form

The non-responsive design means the 60%+ of visitors on mobile devices cannot use the site. No contact form, no online booking, no quote request functionality. The "Not Secure" warning makes visitors leave immediately.

Online Reputation (2/10)

No Reviews on Site, "Closed" Perception

No reviews or testimonials on the website. The "Closed" status actively undermines any existing reputation. No social media activity to counter this perception.
