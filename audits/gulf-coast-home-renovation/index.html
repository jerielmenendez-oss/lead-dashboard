Executive Summary

Gulf Coast Home Renovation has a website, which puts you ahead of many local contractors. But the site is doing more harm than good in its current state.

The GoDaddy Website Builder template looks dated (2018-era design), there are placeholder or missing images on the site, the navigation is cluttered with too many menu items, and the content is stuffed with keywords in a way that Google actually penalizes. Your phone number is a Florida area code (850) even though you’re in Foley, Alabama — which confuses local customers. The site positions you as “most affordable,” but a cheap-looking website undermines that message by making the business look unreliable.

Key Findings

Keyword Stuffing — Google Penalizes This

Your pages repeat the same keywords unnaturally throughout the content. Google’s algorithm detects keyword stuffing and actively demotes pages that do it. Instead of ranking higher, this technique pushes you lower. Modern SEO uses natural language with keywords placed strategically, not crammed into every sentence.

Missing or Placeholder Images

Several images on your site appear to be missing or broken. A remodeling website with missing images is like a restaurant menu with no food descriptions — customers can’t see what they’re getting. For a renovation contractor, project photos are your most powerful sales tool.

Cluttered Navigation

Your site has too many menu items, making it hard for visitors to find what they need. Simplifying to 5–6 clear menu items (Home, Services, Gallery, About, Contact) would improve user experience and keep visitors on the site longer.

Florida Phone Number in Alabama

Your phone number starts with (850), which is a Florida area code. Customers in Foley and Baldwin County may hesitate to call what looks like an out-of-state business. Consider getting a local (251) number, or at minimum make your Foley address extremely prominent so locals know you’re nearby.

Dated 2018-Era Design

Your GoDaddy template looks several years old. Web design trends move fast — a dated site signals to visitors that the business may not be active or up to date. Modern homeowners expect a clean, professional look.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Rebuild the Website with Clean Content

Highest Priority

Replace the GoDaddy template with a clean, modern website. Remove all keyword stuffing and write natural, helpful content. Fix or replace missing images with real photos of your renovation projects. Simplify the navigation. Your “most affordable” positioning needs a professional-looking site to back it up.

2. Add Real Project Photos & Before/Afters

Highest Priority

Replace missing images with real before-and-after photos of your best renovation work. Organize them by project type: kitchen remodels, bathroom remodels, room additions, etc. These photos are what convince homeowners to pick up the phone.

3. Get a Local (251) Phone Number

Medium Priority

A local phone number builds trust with Baldwin County customers. You can forward a (251) number to your existing phone. Display it prominently on your website and Google Business Profile alongside your Foley address.
