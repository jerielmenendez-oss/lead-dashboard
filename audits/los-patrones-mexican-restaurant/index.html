Executive Summary

Los Patrones Mexican Restaurant is already doing something many no-website businesses are not: they offer delivery. That is a significant service that customers want. But without a website or online ordering platform, customers who want delivery cannot place an order without calling in — and many will not bother. They will open DoorDash, find a competitor, and order from them instead. The 34 Yelp reviews indicate real customer engagement, which means the audience is there. The gap is converting that interest into convenient, digital-friendly transactions. Closing that gap is the biggest revenue opportunity available to this business right now.

Key Findings

No Website — Delivery Service Has No Digital Home

Los Patrones offers delivery, but there is no website where customers can see the menu, place an order, or even confirm the restaurant is open. Customers who want delivery today almost universally start with a quick Google or app search. Without a digital order point, you are invisible to most of that demand.

No Online Ordering System

Phone-only ordering creates friction that costs orders. Customers who cannot reach the line during busy hours, prefer not to call, or are ordering from a workplace will abandon the attempt. An online ordering system — even a simple one — can capture orders around the clock without requiring staff to answer the phone.

Facebook Only — No Instagram Presence

Mexican food is visually compelling — tacos, plates of enchiladas, fresh guacamole. Instagram is a natural fit for this cuisine type, and many restaurant customers in Alabama actively follow food accounts. Not having an Instagram presence means missing a free, high-impact visual marketing channel.

No Presence on Delivery Platforms (DoorDash, Uber Eats)

If Los Patrones offers delivery but is not listed on major delivery apps, the business is competing at a serious disadvantage. DoorDash and Uber Eats customers rarely search beyond those platforms for restaurant options. Not being listed there effectively makes you invisible to a large portion of delivery demand.

34 Yelp Reviews — Customers Are Engaging

Yelp reviews demonstrate that customers are finding and rating this restaurant. This is a stronger baseline than many comparable businesses in the area. It signals that when people discover Los Patrones, they like it enough to leave a review — a strong foundation for expanded digital marketing.

Recommendations

1. Set Up Online Ordering

High Priority

Platforms like Toast, Square Online, or ChowNow allow restaurants to accept online orders with minimal setup cost. Alternatively, getting listed on DoorDash or Uber Eats puts you in front of customers already looking to order delivery right now. This is the highest-revenue opportunity available — online ordering removes friction and captures customers who will not call in.

2. Build a Website with a Full Menu

High Priority

A website that shows your full menu — with prices — gives customers the confidence to choose Los Patrones over the competitor whose menu they can already see. Include your hours, phone number, address, and a note that delivery is available. Link to your online ordering system once it is live. This is the digital storefront your delivery service needs.

3. Optimize Your Yelp Profile

High Priority

Claim your Yelp business page and ensure all information is accurate and complete. Add photos, update the menu, and respond to reviews — both positive and negative. Yelp already has traction for this business. A fully optimized profile turns that existing traffic into more customers.

4. Start an Instagram Account

Medium Priority

Post photos of your most popular dishes weekly. Mexican food photographs beautifully and performs well on Instagram. Tag Bay Minette and local area hashtags to increase visibility. A consistent Instagram presence builds brand recognition and reminds followers to order when they are hungry.

5. Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

Medium Priority

Make sure your GBP is verified and fully filled out — including hours, delivery availability, photos, and menu. Google Maps is where most people decide where to eat or order from on a given day. A complete profile with delivery information prominently listed gives you a significant advantage in local search results.
