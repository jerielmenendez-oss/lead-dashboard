Executive Summary

Magnolia Construction & Design LLC builds energy-efficient custom homes across Baldwin County — from Gulf Shores and Orange Beach to Fairhope, Foley, and beyond. You’re a licensed home builder (#25633), a member of NAHB, the Home Builders Association of Alabama, and the Baldwin County HBA. You’ve been in business since 2016 and your pricing ($130–$250/sq ft) puts you in the sweet spot for the market.

Here’s the problem: when someone in Baldwin County searches “custom home builder Gulf Shores” or “energy efficient homes Fairhope,” they’ll find Sanderson Builders, ARK Builders, Green Coast Living, and a dozen other competitors — all of whom have websites. They won’t find you. Your Houzz profile has zero photos and zero reviews. Your BBB listing exists but has no reviews either. You have Facebook and Instagram accounts, but without a website, Google has nothing to index.

Key Findings

No Website Despite Serving 12+ Cities

Magnolia Construction has no website. Your BBB listing references a domain (magnoliallc.net) that does not appear to be active. When you serve Gulf Shores, Orange Beach, Foley, Fairhope, Daphne, Spanish Fort, Summerdale, Robertsdale, Loxley, Elberta, Lillian, and Perdido Key, each of those cities is a keyword opportunity lost. A competitor with a website has a page for “custom homes Gulf Shores” — you have nothing.

No Google Business Profile Found

We could not find a Google Business Profile for Magnolia Construction & Design. This means you’re not appearing in Google Maps or the local search results that get the most clicks. For a custom home builder, this is where serious buyers start their search.

Houzz Profile is Empty

You have a Houzz profile — which is one of the top platforms for home builders — but it has zero photos, zero reviews, and only 7 followers. Your pricing is listed ($130–$250/sq ft) and your affiliations are shown, but without project photos and reviews, this profile is doing nothing for you. Houzz explicitly says “You could be the first review.”

Zero Reviews on Any Platform

After 9 years in business, there are zero public reviews anywhere — not on Houzz, BBB, Google, Yelp, or Angi. For custom home building, where the average project costs $200K+, reviews are critical. Homeowners will not sign a contract with a builder they can’t research online.

Strong Professional Affiliations

Membership in NAHB, the Home Builders Association of Alabama, and the Baldwin County HBA is a mark of professionalism that many competitors lack. These should be prominently featured on a website — they tell homeowners you’re serious, established, and connected to the industry.

Energy-Efficient Homes — A Growing Market

Energy efficiency is one of the top priorities for new home buyers in 2026. This is a powerful differentiator that almost no one can find right now. A website with a dedicated page about your energy-efficient building process would capture buyers actively searching for this.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Build a Professional Website with City-Specific Pages

Highest Priority

Your website should have a homepage showcasing your energy-efficient custom homes, an about page with your affiliations (NAHB, HBAA, Baldwin County HBA) and license info, a gallery of completed projects, and individual pages for your key service areas — especially Gulf Shores, Orange Beach, Fairhope, and Foley. Each city page helps you rank when someone searches “custom home builder [city].” Include your pricing range ($130–$250/sq ft) and a contact form.

2. Claim Your Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Go to business.google.com and create your listing. Set your service area to cover all 12+ cities you serve. Upload photos of completed homes, list your services, and include your license number and association memberships in the description. This is free and will put you on Google Maps immediately.

3. Fill Out Your Houzz Profile & Collect Reviews

Highest Priority

You already have a Houzz profile — it just needs content. Upload your best project photos (aim for 20+), write detailed project descriptions, and reach out to past clients for reviews. Houzz is where high-budget homeowners shop for builders. A complete profile with 5–10 reviews would make you competitive on this platform immediately.
