Key Findings

Domain Is Dead — DNS Resolution Failure

Your domain mpconstructioncontractor.com does not resolve. This means the domain has likely expired or the DNS records are misconfigured. The site simply does not load — not even an error page. Anyone clicking a link to your website (from directories, Google, or business cards) sees nothing.

No professional or personal email address was found for MP Construction. Between the dead website, missing phone, and missing email, there is literally no way for a new customer to reach you online.

10+ City Service Area — Entirely Wasted

You serve cities across Baldwin County, but none of that service area is reflected online. Each city you serve (Foley, Gulf Shores, Fairhope, Daphne, etc.) is a Google search opportunity. A competitor with a website has a page for “contractor Gulf Shores” — you have nothing.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Secure a New Domain & Build a Website

Urgent

Check if mpconstructioncontractor.com is still available to renew. If not, register a new domain (like mpconstructionfoley.com or mpconstruction.co). Build a professional website with your services, the cities you serve, project photos, and most importantly — your phone number and a contact form. Every day without a website is a day you’re invisible to Baldwin County homeowners.

2. Get Your Phone Number Online Everywhere

Urgent

Create a Google Business Profile with your phone number, address, and service area. Claim or create listings on BBB, Yelp, and Angi. The immediate goal is to make sure anyone searching your business name can find a way to call you within 10 seconds.

3. Create City-Specific Landing Pages

Medium Priority

With a 10+ city service area, create a separate page on your website for each major city: Foley, Gulf Shores, Orange Beach, Fairhope, Daphne, Spanish Fort, Robertsdale, Loxley. Each page should target searches like “general contractor [city] AL.” This strategy can put you on page one for multiple cities simultaneously.
