Key Findings

No Website At All

New Star Construction has no website. When a homeowner in Robertsdale, Foley, or Silverhill searches for a general contractor, they find businesses with websites. Without one, you’re not even in the consideration set.

No Google Business Profile

Without a Google Business Profile, you don’t show up on Google Maps. When someone searches “contractor near Robertsdale” or “general contractor Baldwin County,” the map results show your competitors. This is the single most impactful free tool available to any local business.

Limited Online Reviews

Very few online reviews were found for New Star Construction. Reviews are the #1 trust factor for homeowners choosing a contractor. Without them, even if someone finds your name, they have no way to gauge the quality of your work.

No Social Media Presence

No Facebook page, Instagram account, or other social media was found. In the construction industry, before-and-after project photos are incredibly effective at generating inquiries. You’re missing this free marketing channel entirely.

BuildZoom Score of 90 — Proven Track Record

Your BuildZoom score of 90, based on your permit history and license standing, is a strong credential. It tells homeowners that your work is verified through public records. This should be featured prominently on a website and in all marketing materials.

Robertsdale — Central to Baldwin County Growth

Robertsdale is centrally located in Baldwin County, within easy reach of Foley, Fairhope, Daphne, Silverhill, and Loxley. This gives you a natural service area that covers some of the fastest-growing communities in Alabama. A website with pages targeting each of these cities would capture searches across the entire region.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Build a Professional Website with Local Pages

Highest Priority

Create a website with your services, project photos, your BuildZoom credentials, and a contact form. Include dedicated pages for each city you serve (Robertsdale, Foley, Fairhope, Silverhill, Loxley) so you can rank for searches in each area. Your central Baldwin County location is a strategic advantage — use it.

2. Create & Optimize Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Set up your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. List your service area across Baldwin County, add project photos, and include your BuildZoom score in your description. This is free and will immediately put you on the Google Maps for local contractor searches.

3. Start Collecting Google Reviews

Medium Priority

Reach out to past clients and ask for Google reviews. Provide them with a direct link (which your Google Business Profile generates). Going from zero reviews to 10+ reviews changes the entire perception of your business online. Make it a habit to ask every satisfied client for a review when the project wraps up.
