Prioritized Recommendations

1. URGENT: Investigate and Fix Googlebot Noindex Tag Critical Impact

What's wrong: A noindex meta tag directed at googlebot was detected, which may be telling Google not to index the entire site.
Why it matters: If active, this means the site is COMPLETELY INVISIBLE in Google search results. No amount of other SEO work matters until this is fixed.
How to fix: Inspect the page source for <meta name="googlebot" content="noindex"> and remove it immediately. Verify in Google Search Console that pages are being indexed.
Estimated impact: If the site is currently de-indexed, fixing this alone could restore all search visibility overnight.

2. Fix Broken Images High Impact

What's wrong: Multiple image tags are broken or using lazy-loading that Googlebot may not execute.
Why it matters: Broken images make the site look unprofessional and prevent Google from understanding visual content.
How to fix: Audit all images, fix broken src attributes, ensure lazy-loaded images have proper noscript fallbacks, and add alt text.
Estimated impact: Improves user trust, page quality signals, and image search visibility.

3. Remove Duplicate Weebly Site High Impact

What's wrong: A secondary Weebly site exists, creating a confusing dual web presence.
Why it matters: Two sites split link authority, confuse Google about the canonical source, and may cause duplicate content penalties.
How to fix: Take down the Weebly site and 301 redirect any traffic to personaledgefitness.com. Update all directory listings.
Estimated impact: Consolidates all SEO authority into one domain for stronger rankings.

4. Add Social Media Links and Profiles Medium Impact

What's wrong: Zero social media links on the website.
Why it matters: Fitness clients research trainers extensively. Social media provides proof of expertise, community, and results.
How to fix: Create/optimize Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube profiles. Add links to the website header/footer. Post client transformations and workout tips.
Estimated impact: Builds trust signals and provides additional discovery channels for potential clients.

5. Add og:image for Social Sharing Moderate Impact

What's wrong: No og:image tag means shared links on social media appear without a preview image.
Why it matters: Links shared without images get dramatically lower engagement and click-through on social platforms.
How to fix: Create a branded 1200x630px image and add it as the og:image meta tag.
Estimated impact: Social shares become visually compelling — increases referral traffic from social platforms.
