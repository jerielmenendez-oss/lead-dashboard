Executive Summary

S&F Construction LLC specializes in custom decks, fences, and room additions in the Gulf Shores area. These are highly visual services — homeowners want to see photos of your work before they hire you.

But your website at alcoastbuilders.com is essentially non-functional. It contains minimal HTML, no real business content, and appears to use Fillout (a form builder) as the entire site. You also have multiple domain names creating a fragmented online presence. Your Facebook page exists but has only 2 reviews. Homeowners searching for deck builders or fence contractors in Gulf Shores are finding competitors with full portfolios — not a near-blank page with a form.

Key Findings

Website Is Essentially Empty

Your website at alcoastbuilders.com has barely any content. No project photos, no service descriptions, no about section, no contact information displayed. It appears to just be a Fillout form embedded on a blank page. This is worse than having no website — it looks like the business is abandoned or not serious.

Tested: https://alcoastbuilders.com/ → minimal HTML, form builder only

Four Competing Domains Cannibalizing Each Other

We found at least four active domains associated with your business: alcoastbuilders.com, sfconstructional.com, sfcconstructionservices.com, and sfconstructionllc.org. Google can’t determine which is your real site, so it doesn’t rank any of them well. This is actively hurting you. Pick one domain, build a real site there, and redirect all the others to it.

No Project Portfolio Online

Custom decks, fences, and room additions are visual work. Homeowners make hiring decisions based on seeing completed projects. Without photos of your work online, you’re asking customers to take a leap of faith — and most won’t.

Only 2 Facebook Reviews

Your Facebook page has only 2 reviews and is “not yet rated.” For a construction company, a thin review profile makes it hard for homeowners to trust you. Competitors with 20–50 reviews will always get the call first.

Gulf Shores Location — High-Demand Market

Gulf Shores is one of the fastest-growing residential markets in Alabama. Demand for custom decks, fences, and room additions is strong year-round. A professional web presence in this market would generate a steady stream of leads.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Build a Real Website with a Project Gallery

Highest Priority

Replace the empty page with a professional website. It should have: a homepage showcasing your best work, separate service pages for decks, fences, and room additions (each with before-and-after photos), an about page, and a contact form with your phone number. Consolidate all your domains under one — pick the best name and redirect the others.

2. Claim Google Business Profile & Collect Reviews

Highest Priority

Create or claim your Google Business Profile targeting Gulf Shores, Orange Beach, and Foley. Upload your best project photos. Then start actively asking every client for a Google review. Going from 2 reviews to 15–20 would transform your online reputation.

3. Start Posting on Instagram

Medium Priority

Decks, fences, and room additions are Instagram-perfect content. Take photos during and after every project. Post before-and-after comparisons. Tag Gulf Shores and Orange Beach locations. In the visual trades, Instagram is the cheapest, most effective marketing channel available.
