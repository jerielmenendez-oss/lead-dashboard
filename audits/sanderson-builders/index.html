Executive Summary

Sanderson Builders is a four-generation family business based in Elberta. You build custom homes and design swimming pools — high-value services that attract premium clients. That kind of heritage and expertise is rare and marketable.

Your website, however, is stuck in time. It appears to be built with plain HTML from the early 2000s era — no content management system, no mobile responsiveness, and no HTTPS. Modern browsers display a “Not Secure” warning for your site. Over 60% of web traffic comes from phones, and your site doesn’t work on mobile devices. The image rotation feature likely breaks in modern browsers. For a builder whose average project probably costs $200K+, this website is actively undermining your credibility.

Key Findings

No HTTPS — Browser Shows “Not Secure”

Your site runs on HTTP only. Every major browser now shows “Not Secure” in the address bar for HTTP sites. For a homeowner about to invest hundreds of thousands of dollars in a custom home, seeing “Not Secure” on your website is an immediate red flag. Google also ranks HTTPS sites higher than HTTP sites.

Tested: http://www.sandersonbuilders.com/ → No HTTPS available

Not Mobile Responsive — Broken on Phones

Your website does not adapt to phone or tablet screens. Over 60% of web visitors are on mobile devices. When someone pulls up your site on their phone, they see a desktop layout crammed into a tiny screen — text too small to read, images overlapping, and navigation impossible to use. Many will leave immediately.

Early 2000s Design — Looks Abandoned

Your site is built with plain HTML in a style that was common 20 years ago. There’s no modern CMS (WordPress, Squarespace, etc.), which means updates require coding knowledge. The design signals to visitors that the business hasn’t been active recently — even though it has.

Image Rotation Likely Broken

Your site appears to have an image slideshow feature that may rely on outdated JavaScript or Flash. Modern browsers have dropped support for many older web technologies, so this feature likely doesn’t work anymore — showing a blank space or broken images instead of your best work.

Title Tag is 222 Characters of Keyword Stuffing

Your homepage title tag is 222 characters long — an unreadable dump of every city in South Alabama. Google truncates titles at about 60 characters and actively penalizes keyword-stuffed titles. This is hurting your search rankings instead of helping them.

BuildZoom Score of 99 — Completely Wasted

Your BuildZoom score is 99 (top 15% in Alabama), but your Houzz profile has zero photos and zero reviews, and your overall web presence is so outdated that this credential is invisible. This should be a centerpiece of your marketing.

No Contact Form — Just a Link

Your “Contact Us” page appears to be just a link rather than an actual contact form. Modern homeowners expect to be able to send a message directly from the website. Without a form, you’re losing the customers who prefer to reach out digitally before making a phone call.

Four-Generation Family Legacy

A four-generation building legacy is an extraordinary story and trust signal. Very few competitors can claim this kind of heritage. This should be the centerpiece of your website and marketing — “Four generations of building Baldwin County homes.”

Swimming Pool Design — High-Value Service

Pool design and construction is a premium service with high margins. A dedicated “Swimming Pools” page on a modern website would capture homeowners searching “pool builder Baldwin County” or “custom pool Gulf Shores.” This is a keyword opportunity most of your general contractor competitors don’t have.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Rebuild the Website from Scratch

Highest Priority

Your current site cannot be patched — it needs a full rebuild on a modern platform. The new site should be mobile-responsive, HTTPS-secured, and feature your four-generation story prominently. Create separate pages for custom homes, swimming pools, and your service area. Include a project gallery with your best builds and a real contact form. Your family’s 4-generation legacy deserves a website that matches.

2. Optimize Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Make sure your Google Business Profile is fully optimized with photos of completed homes and pools, your service area, and updated business hours. Link it to your new website once it’s live. Start asking clients for Google reviews — a builder with 15+ five-star reviews will dominate local search in Elberta.

3. Create a Portfolio Page Showcasing Pools & Homes

Medium Priority

Your most powerful sales tool is photos of completed projects. Create a portfolio page organized by project type: custom homes, swimming pools, renovations. Include the city/area for each project to boost local SEO. For pool projects especially, professional photos of finished pools are incredibly compelling and will differentiate you from competitors who don’t offer this service.
