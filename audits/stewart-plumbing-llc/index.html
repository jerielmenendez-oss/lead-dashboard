Executive Summary

Stewart Plumbing LLC is a South Baldwin Chamber of Commerce member and BBB-listed plumber in Foley. These are credibility markers that show you’re an established, legitimate business.

But your website at stewarts.services has a broken SSL/TLS certificate, which means every visitor sees a browser security warning. Most people — especially homeowners about to trust someone with their plumbing — will hit the back button immediately when they see “Your connection is not private” or “This site is not secure.” Google also penalizes sites with broken security certificates, pushing you lower in search results. The good news: this is a fixable problem with a clear path to a much better online presence.

Key Findings

Broken SSL Certificate — Security Warning Displayed

Your website at stewarts.services has a broken SSL/TLS certificate. Every browser — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge — displays a full-page security warning before visitors can even see your content. Most people will not click past this warning. This is the most damaging technical issue a website can have.

Tested: https://stewarts.services/ → SSL/TLS handshake failure

Unusual Domain Extension (.services)

The .services domain extension is uncommon and harder for customers to remember or type correctly. Most people expect .com. When someone hears “stewarts dot services” they may type .com instead and land on a completely different website — or nothing at all.

Google Penalizing Broken HTTPS

Google explicitly uses HTTPS as a ranking signal. A site with broken SSL gets demoted in search results. Combined with the security warning that causes visitors to bounce, your site is being penalized twice — once by Google’s algorithm and once by human behavior.

South Baldwin Chamber of Commerce Member

Being a Chamber member adds credibility and often provides a backlink from the Chamber’s website to yours — which helps SEO. This is an asset that should be leveraged on a working website with the Chamber badge displayed prominently.

NAP Inconsistencies Across Directories

Your business appears under different city listings across the internet — Foley, Wetumpka, Summerdale, and even San Antonio, TX. These inconsistent listings confuse Google about where you’re actually located, which directly hurts your local search rankings. These duplicate/incorrect listings need to be cleaned up.

Mixed Reviews on Yelp (3.4 Stars)

Your Yelp listing shows approximately 3.4 stars from 33 reviews, with pricing complaints appearing prominently. A 3.4 rating makes it harder to compete against plumbers with 4.5+ stars. You can’t remove legitimate reviews, but a working website with transparent pricing and testimonials helps offset them.

BBB A+ Rated & Licensed

Your BBB A+ rating, owner Austin Stewart’s licensing (AL #0843 and #5616), and business establishment in 2017 provide strong credibility. BBB profiles rank well in Google. These credentials should be featured on a working website.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Fix or Replace Your Website Immediately

Urgent

The broken SSL certificate is actively driving customers away. You have two options: fix the SSL certificate on your current site (if the hosting provider supports it), or build a new website on a professional platform with automatic SSL. A new site on a .com domain (like stewartplumbingfoley.com) would also solve the unusual domain extension issue. Include your services, service area, phone number, and BBB/Chamber badges.

2. Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Make sure your Google Business Profile is claimed and fully optimized with your services, photos, hours, and service area. Link it to your new website once it’s live. Ask satisfied customers to leave Google reviews — reviews are the #1 factor plumbing customers use to choose who to call.

3. Update All Directory Listings

Medium Priority

Once your new website is live, update your BBB listing, Chamber listing, and any other directory profiles to point to the new URL. Consistent information across all platforms (same phone, address, and website URL everywhere) is a major factor in how Google ranks local businesses.
