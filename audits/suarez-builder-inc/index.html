Executive Summary

Suarez Builder Inc. has a solid track record — 47 permitted projects, multiple contractor licenses, and a BuildZoom score of 90. Lawrence, you’ve built credibility through your work over the years. The problem is that none of this is visible to someone searching for a home builder in Lillian or the surrounding Perdido Key area.

Your Yelp listing isn’t even claimed, which means someone else could potentially edit the information on it. You have zero reviews on any platform. No website, no social media, no email address, no contact form. In 2026, homeowners building a $200K+ home want to research their builder online before making a call. If they can’t find anything, they move on to someone they can verify.

Key Findings

No Website At All

Suarez Builder has no website. When a homeowner in Lillian or Perdido Key searches for a home builder, they find competitors with websites showing portfolios, credentials, and contact forms. You simply don’t appear in those results.

Unclaimed Yelp Listing

You have a Yelp listing, but it’s unclaimed. This means you have no control over the photos, description, or business information shown. An unclaimed listing can display inaccurate info and you can’t respond to reviews (if any appear). Claiming it is free and takes minutes.

Zero Reviews on Any Platform

After 47 permitted projects, there are zero public reviews anywhere online — not on Google, Yelp, BuildZoom, or any other platform. For home building, where the average job costs hundreds of thousands of dollars, reviews are absolutely critical. Homeowners will not move forward with a builder they can’t research.

No Social Media or Professional Email

No Facebook, Instagram, or any social media presence was found. No professional email address is publicly available. For a home builder, project photos on social media and a professional email (like info@suarezbuilder.com) are basic expectations.

BuildZoom Score of 90 — Strong Credentials

A BuildZoom score of 90 based on 47 permitted projects and multiple licenses is a strong indicator of a reliable contractor. This is exactly the kind of third-party validation that wins trust with homeowners — but only if they can see it. A website should feature this prominently.

Lillian/Perdido Key Location — Premium Market

Lillian is close to Perdido Key and the Florida border — a premium residential area with high property values and demand for quality home building. Homeowners in this market expect to find a professional web presence when hiring a builder.

Top 3 Things to Fix First

1. Build a Professional Website

Highest Priority

Create a clean, mobile-friendly website showcasing your home building work. Include your license numbers, BuildZoom score, project photos, service area (Lillian, Perdido Key, Gulf Shores, Foley), and a contact form. For a home builder, the website is often the first impression — and right now you don’t have one.

2. Claim All Online Profiles & Create Google Business Profile

Highest Priority

Claim your Yelp listing immediately — it’s currently unclaimed and uncontrolled. Then create a Google Business Profile at business.google.com with your service area, photos, and license credentials. These two actions are free and will make you visible on the two most important search platforms.

3. Start Collecting Reviews from Past Clients

Highest Priority

Reach out to your best past clients and ask them to leave a Google review. Even 5–10 reviews would be transformative — going from zero reviews to having a 4.5+ star rating immediately changes how homeowners perceive your business. Create a simple link from your Google Business Profile and text it to satisfied clients.
