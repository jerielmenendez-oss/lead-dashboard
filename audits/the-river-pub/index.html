Overall Digital Presence Score: 29 / 100

The River Pub technically has a website domain registered — but the site is broken and inaccessible. Combined with no social media presence and no phone number findable online, this business is effectively invisible to any customer searching digitally.

Executive Summary

A broken website can actually be worse than no website at all. When a customer finds theriverpub.net through a Google search or old reference, they land on an error page and likely conclude the business has closed. There is no Facebook, no Instagram, and no phone number available through a standard online search to reassure them otherwise. The River Pub scores slightly higher than a pure no-website business only because a domain exists — but the practical effect on customer discovery is the same: zero. The priority here is rebuilding digital presence from the ground up, starting with a working website and a functional Google Business Profile.

Key Findings

Website is Broken and Inaccessible (theriverpub.net)

The domain theriverpub.net exists but the website does not load. Any customer who finds this URL — through Google, a directory, or word of mouth — encounters an error page. This actively damages the business's credibility, as many visitors will assume the establishment has permanently closed rather than simply having a technical issue.

No Facebook Page

Facebook remains one of the primary ways local bars and pubs in small Alabama towns communicate with their community — posting specials, events, live music, and changes to hours. The River Pub has no Facebook presence at all, eliminating one of the most accessible and community-oriented digital channels available to a local venue.

No Instagram Presence

A pub or bar environment — lighting, drinks, live events, atmosphere — is highly photographable and performs well on Instagram. Without an account, there is no way to showcase the venue visually or reach an audience that discovers bars and restaurants through social browsing.

No Phone Number Findable Online

A customer who wants to call ahead to check hours, ask about an event, or make a reservation cannot easily find a number to call. This is a significant barrier that will cause potential customers to give up and choose a competitor they can more easily reach.

Complete Digital Absence Creates a "Closed" Perception

Taken together — broken website, no social media, no phone number — the overall online signal is that The River Pub may no longer be operating. For any new resident, visitor, or customer who has not been there before, there is no digital evidence that this business is open and active.

Recommendations

1. Fix or Replace the Existing Website Immediately

High Priority

The domain theriverpub.net already exists, which means the hardest step — securing a name — is done. Either repair the existing site or replace it with a clean, simple page that includes: hours, address, phone number, a brief description, and photos of the venue. A broken website is actively telling potential customers to go elsewhere. This needs to be fixed before anything else.

2. Claim and Complete Your Google Business Profile

High Priority

Set up a verified Google Business Profile with a phone number, hours, address, photos, and the website URL once it is working. This is the fastest way to appear in local Google Maps searches and gives people a way to find your contact information even before they reach the website. A complete GBP also directly counteracts the "is this place still open?" concern.

3. Create a Facebook Page

High Priority

Set up a Facebook business page for The River Pub and post immediately — even just a photo of the space and a note that you are open. In Bay Minette's local community, Facebook is where neighbors share recommendations and where regulars follow their favorite spots. A Facebook page also appears in Google search results, adding another positive signal to your digital presence.

4. Ensure a Phone Number is Consistently Listed Everywhere

High Priority

Make sure the same phone number appears on your website, GBP, Facebook page, and any directory listings. Consistency across platforms reinforces your listing's credibility in Google's local search algorithm and ensures customers can always reach you regardless of where they find you online.

5. Add The River Pub to Online Directories

Medium Priority

Submit the business to Yelp, TripAdvisor, and Apple Maps. Directory listings are a quick and free way to establish multiple points of digital presence simultaneously. They also strengthen your Google search rankings by creating consistent name, address, and phone number citations across the web.
